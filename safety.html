Child Safety & Community Guidelines

Students should learn in a respectful, protected environment.

Last updated: July 13, 2026. These guidelines explain expectations for student safety, respectful behavior, adult oversight, organization participation, and AI-assisted feedback.

Legal review note: This policy should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel and child safety specialists before being treated as a final safeguarding policy.

Core Safety Principles

  • Students should be treated with dignity and respect.
  • AI is a coach, not a judge.
  • Parents and guardians should have appropriate visibility.
  • Organization access must be limited to assigned students.
  • Safety concerns should be reported and reviewed.
  • No student should be pressured to share unnecessary personal information.

Expected Student Conduct

Students must be respectful to other students, parents, mentors, organization staff, and administrators; submit original or properly credited work; use appropriate language; follow session and organization rules; and report safety concerns or uncomfortable interactions.

Prohibited Conduct

  • Bullying, harassment, threats, intimidation, or humiliation.
  • Hate speech or discriminatory conduct.
  • Sexual, exploitative, violent, self-harm, or illegal content.
  • Sharing private information about another person without permission.
  • Impersonation or false parent/organization authorization.
  • Attempts to bypass security or access another student's records.
  • Uploading malware, spam, or unsafe files.
  • Misuse of AI feedback to shame, label, or unfairly judge students.

Adult and Administrator Responsibilities

Master Admins, organization administrators, mentors, coaches, teachers, and moderators should keep student safety first, access only information needed for their role, avoid unnecessary one-on-one unsupervised interactions, report and escalate safety concerns, review student submissions fairly, and avoid public shaming or unnecessary comparison between students.

Legal review note: Formal adult conduct, mandatory reporting, and background-check policies are not fully implemented in code and need operational/legal review.

Organization Safety

Organization administrators may manage only their assigned organization. They may invite students, link existing YUVA students, manage groups, assign coaches, teachers, or moderators, and archive or transfer memberships. Organization administrators may not delete global student identities or access students outside their organization.

AI Safety

AI-assisted feedback should be encouraging, age-appropriate, educational, and reviewed with human judgment. AI should not be used to diagnose, shame, rank unfairly, or make final decisions about a student. Students should not submit highly sensitive personal information to AI-assisted practice workflows.

Upload and Presentation Safety

Students may upload research or presentation files for learning review. Uploaded files should be relevant to educational activities and should not contain unsafe, private, copyrighted, or inappropriate material.

Presentation recordings may be used only if enabled and properly consented to.

Legal review note: Final recording and media review procedures need to be approved before recording workflows are expanded.

Safety Reporting

Students can submit safety reports through the student dashboard. Parents may contact YUVA Club support. YUVA Club may review reports, restrict access, contact parents or organizations, preserve records, or take other action to protect students.

Enforcement

YUVA Club may remove content, restrict access, suspend accounts, archive organization membership, notify parents or organizations, or take other action when these guidelines are violated.

Emergencies

YUVA Club is not an emergency service. If there is immediate danger, contact local emergency services.

Legal review note: Add jurisdiction-appropriate crisis and safeguarding resources before final publication.
